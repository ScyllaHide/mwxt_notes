here is a way through ringmodulation to transform arbitrary waves into new ones containing only odd harmonics (somewhat hollow, clean, aetherical sounding):

(since ringmod is the multiplikation of 2 audio-signals, both oscillators can be threated the same way, unlike in FM, where one is the carrier, the other one the modulator. despite of that in this text i call the ringmodulating oscs "carrier" and "modulator". you can swap them. take in the MW2 osc1 as carrier, or osc2 if you prefer)
to generate odd harmonics, you have to follow the following rules:

1. you can select the carrier waveform arbitrary. (also waves with even harmonics, like sawtooth)
2. the modulator osc waveform must consist of odd harmonics only. (i.e. triangle, square, sine)
3. the frequency ratio must be in the form:
    fC : fM = 2m : 2n-1 (m,n = 1, 2, 3, …)
    means: fC : fM = even : odd
4. the ringmod-signal must be the only one turned up in the mixer of the osc-section.

example: in the mw2/XT(k) set osc2 1 oktave deeper than osc1, select triangle as wave2, wavetable 64 (chorus2) and make a wavetable-sweep on wave1.

the table maps every freq-ratio betw. carrier and modulator to the pitch offset of modulator relative to carrier.
example for using the table (microwave2):
to get a ringmod ratio of fC : fM = 2 : 13 look up in the table and set the pitch of the modulator by 3 octaves, -4 semitones and +52 detune units higher than the carrier.)
