also in this series: all frequency ratios and ringmodulation odd harmonics

to generate only odd harmonics by osc- and/or filter FM, you have to follow the following rules:
1. you can select the modulator waveform arbitrary. (also waves with even harmonics, like sawtooth)
2. the carrier osc waveform must consist of odd harmonics only. (i.e. triangle, square, sine)
3. the frequency ratio must be in the form:
fC : fM = (2m-1) : 2n (m,n = 1, 2, 3, …)
means: fC : fM = odd : even
4. the carrier-signal must be the only one turned up in the mixer of the osc-section.

(these rules are reversed to those of odd harmonic ringmod)

example (mw2/XT(k) or Q):
we make both an osc-FM: osc2 -->fm --> osc1
and a filter-FM: osc2 --> filter cutoff freq.
set osc2 1 octave higher than osc1 (fC : fM = 1 : 2), select triangle or square as wave1 and modulate fm amount or cutoff.

XT: wavetable 5 (square sweep) is good as carrier and modulator. as carrier, you can gradually change from clean to buzzy. as modulator, the upper waves (near square) sound more squechy, brighter, whereas the lower waves (near sine) make the fm darker and somewhat noisier.

the table maps every freq-ratio betw. carrier and modulator to the pitch offset of modulator relative to carrier.
example for using the table (microwave2):
to get a FM ratio of fC : fM = 2 : 13 look up in the table and set the pitch of the modulator by 3 octaves, -4 semitones and +52 detune units higher than the carrier.
